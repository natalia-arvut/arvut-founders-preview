Approval covers: (a) the campaign as such; (b) public amplification of the «besten» superlative — Federico's UWG view requested (the page frames «best» as five verifiable criteria, not a rating — see appendix).

Requested: approve launch; Federico's UWG nod on record.

2Decision 2 — Lead distribution rule

Who receives /best leads while the network is small (today: one paying pilot, one anchor candidate, plus Arvut GmbH's own fiduciary entity)?

Proposed rule:

  1. Match by canton → language → industry/size. Where several fiduciaries qualify, rotation.
  2. Backstop: if no network fiduciary can take a lead (canton/language/capacity), it goes to Arvut GmbH's own fiduciary entity — no lead is dropped. The backstop is temporary by design: as the network grows, its share goes to zero.
  3. A lead handed to a fiduciary is exclusive to that fiduciary (no parallel shopping of the same lead).
  4. Every handover is logged (lead source, date, fiduciary, outcome) — this log is also the billing basis for Decision 3.
Requested: confirm the rule; agree who maintains the log (proposal: Alex, reviewed at each board meeting).

3Decision 3 — Client Acquisition Fee (CAF), board-level canon

CHF 500 one-time per introduced client, payable by the fiduciary at mandate signing (not per raw lead). Covers Arvut's acquisition cost. Market-tested: this is the exact price fiduciaries historically paid Alex in his manual practice.

Positioning in the model: third layer, on top of platform production pricing (CHF 500/year per mandate + CHF 1.50/booking) — an introduced client generates both the one-time fee and recurring production revenue.

Visibility (already fixed in messaging canon): candidate letters and sales conversations — yes; public site, /pricing, SME-facing surfaces — no; investor materials — not without a separate decision (adding a third revenue stream means rebuilding the FP; deck v19 stays as is).

Requested: confirm CAF as canon; mandate Federico to draft the «Client Acquisition» annex to the SoW (fee trigger, definition of an introduced client, exclusivity of a handed-over lead).

4Decision 4 — Sales resource: roles and ownership

Heinz's two hires close the TOFU/MOFU gap: they generate and qualify conversations, they do not sell. Escalation: product/demo → Alex; senior owners, M&A tone → Heinz. Weekly 15-min sync (Heinz + Alex) on the touch log.

Requested: confirm this division; decide whose calendar receives the 20-min meetings (Calendly owner); note the dependency — production capacity (Yulia/Tetiana) must be re-checked before we accelerate spend on lead generation, so that converted leads don't hit a delivery wall.

AAppendix — guardrails already in force

«Best» as a checklist, not a rating

The page explicitly converts the superlative into five verifiable criteria (real-time books; every figure reconciled to source; branded client portal; Swiss hosting, revDSG, Art. 958f OR; one platform, one standard). This is the UWG defense line.

Pricing visibility

Fiduciaries see prices; SMEs never do. No figures in LinkedIn DMs — pricing lives in the call.

Honest-status rule

Mechanism claims allowed («we build a public demand channel», «founder-proven method»); flow claims forbidden («leads are already streaming») until real /best leads exist.

Anonymization

All proof points in outreach are anonymized («Möbelhändler», «Gastro-Betrieb»).
