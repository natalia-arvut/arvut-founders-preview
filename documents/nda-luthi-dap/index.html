ABackground & Purpose

Arvut develops and operates AI-assisted accounting and financial software and has conceived and owns a proprietary management-accounting technology known as the Digital Accounting Protocol («DAP»), which is the subject of ongoing intellectual-property protection efforts. In order to explore and, as the case may be, pursue a possible collaboration, advisory role, financing or other business relationship between the Parties (the «Purpose»), Arvut is prepared to disclose to the Receiving Party certain confidential and proprietary information, including in respect of DAP. This Agreement governs the treatment of that information.

1Confidential Information

  1. Definition. «Confidential Information» means all information disclosed by or on behalf of Arvut to the Receiving Party, whether before or after the date of this Agreement, in any form (oral, written, electronic, visual or otherwise) and whether or not marked as confidential, that relates to Arvut, its group, its business, technology, clients or affairs.
  2. DAP. Confidential Information expressly includes, without limitation, all information concerning DAP — its concept, architecture, logic, finite-state model and transition rules, structural invariants and reconciliation identities, the two-projection / delta-layer method, mapping rules, heuristics, data feeds, predictive components, source code, algorithms, data structures, models, demonstrations, documentation and roadmap.
  3. IP status. Confidential Information further includes the existence, content, scope and status of any invention disclosure, patent or other intellectual-property application, filing strategy or priority claim relating to DAP or to any other Arvut technology.
  4. Business information. Confidential Information further includes Arvut's business, financial, commercial, product, pricing, personnel, investor and client information, know-how and trade secrets, and the fact and content of the Parties' discussions and of this Agreement.

2Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by written records: (a) was lawfully in its possession, free of any obligation of confidence, before disclosure by Arvut; (b) is or becomes generally available to the public other than through any act or omission of the Receiving Party; (c) is lawfully received from a third party entitled to disclose it without restriction; or (d) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without any use of or reference to the Confidential Information.

3Undertakings of the Receiving Party

The Receiving Party shall:

  1. use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and for no other purpose whatsoever;
  2. keep the Confidential Information strictly secret and confidential, and protect it with at least the degree of care it applies to its own confidential information of like importance, and in no event less than reasonable care;
  3. not disclose, publish or otherwise make available any Confidential Information to any third party without Arvut's prior written consent;
  4. not copy, reproduce or record any Confidential Information except to the extent strictly necessary for the Purpose;
  5. not make any public statement or announcement concerning DAP, the Confidential Information or the existence and content of the Parties' discussions.

4No Reverse Engineering; No Competing Use

The Receiving Party shall not, directly or indirectly, reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble or otherwise attempt to derive the underlying ideas, methods, structure or source code of DAP or of any other technology comprised in the Confidential Information, nor use the Confidential Information to design, develop, procure or operate any product, method or service that competes with, replicates or is derived from DAP or Arvut's technology.

5Intellectual Property; No Adverse Filing

  1. All Confidential Information, and all intellectual-property rights in DAP and in Arvut's technology, remain the exclusive property of Arvut. No licence or right, express or implied, is granted to the Receiving Party except the limited right to use the Confidential Information for the Purpose.
  2. The Receiving Party shall not, directly or indirectly, apply for, file, register or acquire, or assist any third party to apply for, file, register or acquire, any patent, utility model, design, trademark, domain or other intellectual-property right that is based on, incorporates or is derived from the Confidential Information, nor challenge, oppose or seek to invalidate any of Arvut's intellectual-property rights relating to DAP.

6Compelled Disclosure

If the Receiving Party is required to disclose Confidential Information by law, regulation or binding order of a competent authority or court, it shall (to the extent legally permitted) promptly notify Arvut in writing before disclosure, disclose only the minimum required, and cooperate with Arvut's reasonable efforts to obtain confidential treatment or a protective order.

7Return & Destruction

Upon Arvut's written request, and in any event upon termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly return or, at Arvut's option, irretrievably destroy all Confidential Information and all copies, notes and materials embodying it, and confirm such destruction in writing. The confidentiality obligations under this Agreement survive any such return or destruction.

8Term & Survival

This Agreement takes effect on the date stated above and continues until terminated by either Party on written notice. The Receiving Party's confidentiality and non-use obligations survive termination and remain in force for a period of five (5) years thereafter; provided that, in respect of Confidential Information that constitutes a trade secret, such obligations continue for as long as the information remains a trade secret. The obligations under this Agreement are in addition to, and do not replace or limit, any confidentiality, fiduciary or non-competition duties the Receiving Party may owe to Arvut on any other basis.

9No Warranty; No Obligation to Proceed

The Confidential Information is provided «as is», without warranty of any kind. Nothing in this Agreement obliges either Party to disclose any particular information, to proceed with the Purpose, or to enter into any further agreement.
