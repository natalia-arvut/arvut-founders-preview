1. Purpose and Confidential Information

The Receiving Party is evaluating, and potentially negotiating, a business collaboration with Arvut GmbH under which the Receiving Party would use Arvut's platform, methodology and related services in its own fiduciary business and for its clients (the "Purpose"). For this Purpose, the Disclosing Party will disclose confidential information, in particular concerning DAP — the Digital Accounting Protocol: its concept, methodology, algorithms, documentation, demonstrations, product roadmap, financial details and any related technical or commercial materials (together the "Confidential Information"), whether disclosed in written, electronic, oral or visual form. Oral or visual disclosures (including demonstrations) are covered if identified as confidential at the time of disclosure or if their confidential nature is apparent from the circumstances. The existence, status and content of the Parties' discussions also constitute Confidential Information of both Parties; neither Party shall make any public announcement relating thereto without the other Party's prior written consent.

2. Obligations of the Receiving Party

The Receiving Party shall:

a) use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose;

b) keep the Confidential Information strictly confidential and protect it with at least the same care as its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care;

c) disclose it only to its directors, officers, employees and professional advisors who need it for the Purpose and who are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Agreement or by statutory professional secrecy; the Receiving Party is liable for any breach by such persons as for its own breach (Art. 101 CO);

d) not use, implement or replicate Confidential Information relating to DAP, in whole or in part, in the design, development or provision of its own or third-party products or services (non-use). This Agreement does not restrict the Receiving Party from carrying on its ordinary business, including accounting or fiduciary services, on the basis of information falling under Section 3 or of general professional skills and experience, provided this occurs without use of the Confidential Information. For the avoidance of doubt, this Agreement is not a non-compete undertaking;

e) not reverse engineer, decompile or otherwise attempt to derive the DAP methodology, algorithms or underlying logic from any demonstration, software or materials made available.

3. Exceptions

The obligations under Section 2 do not apply to information which the Receiving Party can demonstrate:

a) is or becomes publicly available without breach of this Agreement;

b) was lawfully known to it before disclosure, free of any confidentiality obligation;

c) was independently developed by persons without access to the Confidential Information, as evidenced by contemporaneous written records; or

d) must be disclosed under mandatory law or by order of a competent authority — in which case the Receiving Party shall, where legally permitted, notify the Disclosing Party without delay and limit the disclosure to the required minimum.

4. No Licence, No Obligation, No Warranty

No intellectual property rights or licences are granted or transferred under this Agreement. Any use of Arvut's platform, methodology or services beyond evaluation for the Purpose shall take place only under a separately executed written agreement (e.g. a pilot, partnership or service agreement), which shall exclusively govern such use; this Agreement continues to apply to all Confidential Information not covered by such agreement. Nothing in this Agreement obliges either Party to enter into any collaboration or other transaction. The Confidential Information is provided "as is"; the Disclosing Party makes no representation or warranty as to its accuracy or completeness. The protection of the Disclosing Party's trade secrets under statutory law, in particular the Swiss Unfair Competition Act (UWG) and Art. 162 of the Swiss Criminal Code, remains reserved.

5. Return and Deletion

Upon the Disclosing Party's first request, the Receiving Party shall return or irrevocably delete all Confidential Information, including copies and derived notes, and confirm this in writing. Copies required by mandatory law or internal audit policies, and automatically created backup copies not readily accessible in the ordinary course, may be retained but remain subject to this Agreement for as long as they are retained.

6. Term

This Agreement takes effect upon signature by both Parties. The obligations under Sections 2–5 remain in force for five (5) years from signature. For Confidential Information constituting a trade secret, the obligations under Sections 2(a)–(c) remain in force for as long as such information remains a trade secret; the non-use obligation under Section 2(d) expires five (5) years from signature.

7. Contractual Penalty

In case of breach of Section 2, the Receiving Party shall pay a contractual penalty (Konventionalstrafe, Art. 160 et seq. CO) of CHF 50'000 for each breach; a series of related breaches arising from the same event counts as one breach, and in the case of a continuing breach each commenced month constitutes a separate breach. The penalty is due irrespective of proof of actual damage; the aggregate of penalties under this Section is capped at CHF 250'000. Payment of the penalty does not release the Receiving Party from its obligations under this Agreement; the right to claim damages exceeding the penalty (Art. 161 para. 2 CO) and to seek injunctive relief remains reserved.

8. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This Agreement is governed by substantive Swiss law, excluding its conflict-of-law rules. The courts at the seat of Arvut GmbH (Zug, Switzerland) have exclusive jurisdiction; the Disclosing Party's right to seek interim or injunctive relief before any other competent court remains reserved.

9. Final Provisions

If any provision of this Agreement is held invalid, the remaining provisions remain in force and the invalid provision shall be replaced by a valid provision closest to its economic intent. Amendments require written form.
