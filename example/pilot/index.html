Clause 1 — Subject Matter

Arvut shall provide back-office accounting services to the Fiduciary within the scope of a pilot project concerning one or two mandates, as designated in Annex 1, which forms an integral part of this Agreement.

Clause 2 — Term

The pilot project shall commence on [to be defined] and shall run for a term of 3 months.

Clause 3 — Services

Within the scope of the pilot project, Arvut shall support the Fiduciary in the back-office accounting activities for the companies / mandates listed in Annex 1, including in particular the recording of accounts payable, accounts receivable and cash receipts, bank reconciliations, the preparation of the basis for the VAT return, the entry of data in the VAT portal where access is available, the reconciliation of revenues on the basis of the documents provided, as well as the preparation of accounting records and overviews relating to payroll accounting, social security contributions, withholding tax, accruals, deferrals, depreciation and reporting.

Entries shall be recorded at the level of the individual invoice and voucher / receipt line items, and not merely at the level of the document as a whole.

These activities shall be performed on the basis of the data and documents made available by the Fiduciary and, within the scope of this pilot project and unless otherwise agreed in writing, shall not include any independent final audit, any tax validation or any statutory annual financial statements.

Clause 4 — Remuneration

The remuneration for the pilot project shall be CHF 1'000.00 per mandate, exclusive of VAT. During the pilot phase, Arvut shall process all documents provided without any volume limit.

Clause 5 — Crediting upon Continuation

In the event that the cooperation is continued after the pilot project, the amount of CHF 1'000.00 per mandate shall be credited against the first amount invoiced (base subscription and entries), up to a maximum of CHF 1'000.00 for the respective mandate. If the cooperation is not continued, nothing further shall be owed by the Fiduciary, irrespective of the volume processed during the pilot project.

The standard tariff after the pilot project shall be:

  • CHF 500.-- base subscription per year and per mandate (end client of the Fiduciary implemented in the arvut.ch solution), irrespective of company size.

  • CHF 1.50 per entry, i.e. per record in the journal. Volume discounts shall be granted on the total annual volume of entries across all mandates of the Fiduciary: −5% from 5'000 entries/year, −10% from 10'000 entries/year, to be recognised by way of settlement at year-end. No minimum package is provided for. The complete terms are set out in the SoW document.

Clause 6 — Definition of an Entry

For the purposes of this Agreement, invoicing shall be effected per entry. An entry means a single record in the journal; thus, for example, a bank statement with 100 movements corresponds to 100 entries. Reversals and corrections shall be counted as entries; a set of books kept in a parallel currency (e.g. EUR/CHF) shall be counted only once. The number of files, pages or documents is not in itself decisive: what is decisive is the number of records in the journal. In case of doubt as to the count, in particular for deliveries containing bank statements with multiple movements or in non-standard formats, the parties may agree on the applicable count in writing before processing begins.

Clause 7 — Duties of Cooperation of the Fiduciary

The Fiduciary shall make available to Arvut, in a timely and complete manner, all documents, bank statements, payroll data, proofs of payment and other information required for the performance of the activities provided for in this Agreement.

Arvut shall not be liable for any delays, incompleteness or discrepancies attributable to missing, late, incomplete or incorrect documents supplied by the Fiduciary or by third parties.

Clause 8 — Invoicing and Payment

Arvut shall issue its invoice at the start of the pilot project. Payment shall be due within 10 days of the invoice date. In the event of default, default interest of 5% per annum (Art. 104 of the Swiss Code of Obligations, CO) shall be owed from the due date, plus reasonable reminder costs.

Clause 9 — Confidentiality and Data Protection

The parties undertake to treat all business, accounting, technical and organisational information received within the scope of this Agreement — their own and that of the end clients — as confidential and as business secrets within the meaning of Art. 162 of the Swiss Criminal Code, subject to statutory requirements or the demands of competent authorities. These obligations shall continue to apply for 5 years after termination of the Agreement. Upon termination, and subject to statutory retention obligations, Arvut shall, upon written request, return and/or destroy the remaining copies of the data and confirm this in writing. Arvut may engage auxiliary persons and subcontractors only if they are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations, and shall remain responsible for them (Art. 101 of the Swiss Code of Obligations, CO).

The processing of personal data shall be governed by the Annex — Data Processing (Art. 9 revFADP) of the SoW document, which shall also apply to this pilot project. No processing of real end-client data shall take place before that Annex has been signed.

Clause 10 — Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This Agreement shall be governed by Swiss law.

The exclusive place of jurisdiction shall be Zurich, subject to mandatory provisions to the contrary.

Clause 11 — Final Provisions

Amendments or supplements to this Agreement shall be valid only if made in writing and signed by both parties; this written-form clause itself may likewise be amended only in writing.

The invalidity or unenforceability of any clause shall not affect the validity of the remaining clauses; the parties shall replace the invalid clause with another of equivalent and legally permissible economic effect.

Neither party may assign this Agreement without the written consent of the other, except in the case of an assignment to group companies or in the event of a reorganisation of Arvut. Neither party shall be liable for non-performance due to force majeure (including network outages and non-attributable external events), for the duration thereof. Material notices shall be given in writing (by letter or e-mail to the addresses indicated by the parties). This Agreement may be signed electronically and in several counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original.

Clauses 9 (Confidentiality and Data Protection) and 10 (Governing Law and Jurisdiction), as well as any accrued payment obligation, shall survive the termination of this Agreement.

This Agreement constitutes the entire understanding between the parties with respect to the pilot project described herein and supersedes any prior understanding, communication or proposal with the same subject matter.
