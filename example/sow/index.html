1) Terms and Definitions

Mandate: an end client of the Fiduciary serviced by Arvut under this SoW.

End client: a natural/legal person with whom the Fiduciary maintains a mandate/contractual relationship for accounting services.

Document / voucher / source document: a single document or a single file treated as a unit of processing (e.g. invoice, receipt/voucher, proof of payment, bank statement/bank report, contract/document where required, etc.). Journal entry: a single entry in the general-ledger journal. The service is invoiced per journal entry (see Art. 7), not per document.

White-label: Arvut's services are provided under the branding of the Fiduciary, unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Read-only access: access exclusively for viewing purposes (without editing or entry rights).

The Fiduciary's system (target system): the accounting system used by the Fiduciary for administration/finalisation and closing.

2) Collaboration Model (White-Label, Front-end / Back-end)

Arvut is a managed accounting production service (back-end production) in white-label mode.

The Fiduciary (front-end) is responsible for:

  • the relationship with the end clients and the contracts;
  • communication with authorities/agencies, including filings;
  • VAT filing on the basis of the material prepared by Arvut;
  • annual financial statements, accruals/closing entries, statutory accounts, taxes.

Arvut (back-end) is responsible for:

  • processing of documents and data, posting/journal entries, reconciliations;
  • preparation of the VAT calculation basis and VAT reports;
  • preparation of the reporting packages for the Fiduciary in accordance with this SoW.

Nature of the contract. The parties qualify this agreement as a service contract of a continuing nature (continuing obligation) of mixed character (services + access to the arvut.ch platform) and not as a simple mandate within the meaning of Art. 394 et seq. CO. To the extent that Art. 404 CO were to apply, the parties agree that ordinary termination is permissible only as per the end of the current contractual period, subject to the notice period stated in the header, without prejudice to extraordinary termination for good cause.

Communication (standard): queries are routed Arvut → Fiduciary → end client. Direct contact Arvut ↔︎ end client only with the prior written authorisation of the Fiduciary.

3) Scope of Services (Deliverables) — per Mandate

For each mandate, Arvut provides:

  • Line-item posting: processing of the documents; detailed journal entries per line/item, not merely by totals.
  • Open items and payment reconciliation: maturity schedule/open items for customers and suppliers (AR/AP); allocation and reconciliation of payments on the basis of bank statements/transaction data/payment confirmations.
  • VAT: calculation basis and report, ready for filing by the Fiduciary.
  • Preparation of the annual financial statements (preparation, not legal finalisation): balance sheet / income statement, open items, VAT report in PDF; package ready for review and finalisation by the Fiduciary.
  • Integrated archive (document ↔︎ journal entry): traceable linkage between document and journal entry for fast checks and audit trail.

4) Input and Process

For each mandate, the Fiduciary provides: vouchers, bank statements/transaction data, proofs of payment; where required, cash/expenses.

The SLA periods run from receipt of the complete set for the reporting period.

In the event of missing data, Arvut prepares a query/exception list (exception list) for the Fiduciary.

5) SLA and Corrections

  • Standard processing: up to 2 weeks from receipt of the complete set;
  • Corrections: within 2 business days from notification and confirmation by the Fiduciary.

6) Portal and White-Label Access

  • the Fiduciary has one administrative access per mandate;
  • the end clients may be granted read-only access under the branding of the Fiduciary (white-label), provided this is envisaged in the agreement between the Fiduciary and the client.

7) Prices and Invoicing (per Mandate / Year)

The following annual terms apply per mandate:

  • Base subscription: CHF 500.-- per year and per mandate (end client of the Fiduciary activated in the arvut.ch solution), irrespective of company size.
  • Usage-based fee: CHF 1.50 per journal entry. A journal entry is a single entry in the general-ledger journal; thus, for example, a bank statement with 100 movements corresponds to 100 journal entries. Reversals and corrections are counted as journal entries. A parallel-currency ledger (e.g. EUR/CHF) is counted only once: the derived ledger does not double the count.
  • Volume discounts (on the annual total volume of journal entries across all mandates of the Fiduciary): from 5'000 journal entries/year −5%, from 10'000 journal entries/year −10%. The discount applies only to the usage-based fee, not to the base subscription, and is recognised at year-end by way of settlement (credit note).
  • The base subscription and the journal entries are invoiced periodically; no minimum package and no implementation charge are envisaged. All prices are exclusive of VAT. In the event of late payment, default interest of 5% per annum (Art. 104 CO) is owed from the due date, plus reasonable reminder costs.

8) Handover / Export to the Fiduciary's System

Upon request, Arvut delivers structured exports (CSV/XLS and/or agreed formats) for handover and reconciliation in the system used by the Fiduciary (target system).

In the event of termination of the collaboration, Arvut delivers:

  • export of the movements/data;
  • PDF archive of the documents and of the principal reports;
  • support with the transfer/import into the target system (on a reasonable-effort basis).

9) Liability (Limitation)

Arvut's liability per mandate is limited to the amount of the fees paid by the Fiduciary for that mandate in the 12 months preceding the damaging event (rolling 12 months). This limitation does not apply in the event of intent or gross negligence on the part of Arvut or its auxiliary persons (Art. 100 para. 1 and Art. 101 CO), nor in the cases in which the law prohibits a limitation of liability. In any event, liability for indirect damage, loss of profit and consequential damage is excluded, without prejudice to intent or gross negligence.

10) Early Termination and Setup Indemnity

Subject to the provisions of Art. 2 on the nature of the contract, in the event of early termination by the Fiduciary before the end of the current period, the accrued fees remain owed, together with a lump-sum indemnity to cover the implementation and setup costs in the amount of 3 months' base subscription per active mandate. The indemnity has the character of a lump-sum cost recovery and not of a contractual penalty. Extraordinary termination for good cause attributable to Arvut remains reserved, in which case no indemnity is owed.

11) Annexes (Mandates) and Procedure for Adding Mandates

The list of mandates is administered by way of annexes to this SoW.

  • Annex 1 contains up to 10 mandates.
  • For the addition of further mandates beyond the limit of Annex 1, a new annex is created: Annex 2, then Annex 3 and so on.
  • Each annex forms an integral part of this SoW.
  • The addition of mandates via new annexes does not alter the other terms of the SoW (SLA, prices, operating model), unless expressly stated otherwise in the annex concerned.

12) Guarantee of "No Poaching of Clients" (non-circumvention / non-solicitation) and Contractual Penalty

Arvut acknowledges that all end clients (mandates) serviced under this SoW are clients of the Fiduciary.

12.1 Non-circumvention vis-à-vis the Fiduciary (non-circumvention). Arvut undertakes, for the entire term of the SoW and for 24 months after its termination, not to conclude or perform, whether directly or through third parties/affiliated companies, contracts for accounting or similar services with the end clients of the Fiduciary that were introduced or serviced under this SoW. For the avoidance of doubt: Arvut remains free to offer its services to any other person, fiduciary company or any client not serviced under this SoW.

12.2 Non-solicitation. Arvut undertakes not to engage in any direct marketing, offers, solicitation or other actions aimed at inducing clients of the Fiduciary to enter into a direct contract with Arvut.

12.3 Exception (spontaneous contact by the client). If a client of the Fiduciary spontaneously and directly contacts Arvut, Arvut undertakes: (a) to inform the Fiduciary within a reasonable time; and (b) to continue the interaction exclusively through the Fiduciary, or to conclude a direct contract only with the prior written authorisation of the Fiduciary.

12.4 Contractual penalty (penalty clause). For each established breach of clauses 12.1–12.2 and for each affected mandate, Arvut shall pay the Fiduciary a contractual penalty equal to the value of the service for 24 months for the affected mandate (at the tariff applicable at the time of the breach, understood as the subscription plus the monthly average of the journal entries of the last 12 months), but in any event not less than CHF 5'000.00 per mandate. The parties agree that this amount represents a reasonable, good-faith estimate of the foreseeable prejudice; the power of the court pursuant to Art. 163 para. 3 CO remains reserved.

Payment of the contractual penalty is without prejudice to the right of the Fiduciary to demand cessation of the breach and compensation for demonstrable damage to the extent not covered by the contractual penalty, within the limits permitted by the applicable law (Art. 161 para. 2 CO).

13) Confidentiality and Secrecy

Each party treats all information of the other party and of the end clients as a business secret (cf. Art. 162 SCC). These obligations continue to apply for 5 years after termination of this SoW. Upon termination and subject to statutory retention obligations, Arvut returns the remaining copies of the data upon written request and/or destroys them and confirms this in writing. Arvut may disclose to its auxiliary persons/sub-suppliers only if these are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations, and remains responsible for them (Art. 101 CO). Arvut acts as an auxiliary person of the Fiduciary, and any professional secrecy of the latter remains unaffected (incl. Art. 321 SCC, to the extent applicable); the Fiduciary warrants the necessary authorisations and consents.

14) Final Provisions

  • Written form: amendments and supplements are valid only if agreed by both parties in writing; this written-form clause itself may likewise be amended only in writing.
  • Severability: the possible nullity or ineffectiveness of a clause does not affect the remaining clauses; the parties shall replace it with another one of equivalent and legally permissible economic effect.
  • Assignment: neither party may assign the contract without the written consent of the other, except for assignment to group companies or in the event of a reorganisation of Arvut.
  • Set-off: the Fiduciary may set off only undisputed claims or claims established by a court of law.
  • Force majeure: neither party is liable for non-performance due to force majeure (including network interruptions and non-attributable external events); the SLAs are suspended for its respective duration.
  • Notices: in writing (registered mail or e-mail to the addresses indicated by the parties).
  • Electronic signature: this SoW may be signed electronically and in several counterparts, each of which is deemed an original.
  • Survival: Articles 9 (Liability), 10 (Indemnity), 12 (non-circumvention and contractual penalty), 13 (Confidentiality), 15 (Governing Law and Jurisdiction) as well as the Annex — Data Processing survive termination.

15) Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This SoW is governed by Swiss law. The exclusive place of jurisdiction is Zürich, subject to mandatory provisions to the contrary.

16) Precedence and Replacement of Prior Agreements

This SoW supersedes and entirely replaces any prior agreement, understanding, correspondence, proposal or annex between Arvut and the Fiduciary concerning the subject matter of the back-end accounting services in white-label mode, unless expressly stated otherwise in this SoW or in its annexes. Amendments and supplements are valid only if agreed by both parties in writing.

ANNEX 2 — PROCESSING OF PERSONAL DATA (Art. 9 revFADP/nDSG · SR 235.1)

Data processing agreement. Integral part of the SoW. No processing of real end-client data takes place before this Annex has been signed.

1. Roles and subject matter

  1. The Fiduciary is the controller (controller, Art. 5 lit. j FADP); Arvut is the processor (processor, Art. 5 lit. k and Art. 9 FADP).
  2. Arvut processes the data exclusively for the provision of the services under the SoW and on the documented instructions of the Fiduciary.
  3. The Fiduciary warrants that it is the controller (or an authorised processor) and that it holds the authorisation of the end clients to engage Arvut as (sub-)processor (Art. 9 para. 3 FADP).

2. Categories of data and data subjects

  1. Data subjects: employees, suppliers, customers of the mandates.
  2. Categories: master data, accounting data, bank data, payroll data (incl. AHV/BVG/withholding tax) as well as any sensitive personal data concerning health (e.g. sickness/accident benefits under the AIA/UVG) — Art. 5 lit. c FADP.

3. Instructions and limits

  1. Processing only on instructions; no use for Arvut's own purposes (Art. 9 para. 1 FADP).
  2. Arvut does only what the Fiduciary is itself authorised to do.
  3. Arvut reports any instructions it considers unlawful.

4. Confidentiality and secrecy

  1. Arvut and its personnel are bound to confidentiality, including after termination; Arvut acts as an auxiliary person of the Fiduciary.
  2. Any professional secrecy of the Fiduciary remains unaffected (incl. Art. 321 SCC, to the extent applicable); the Fiduciary warrants the necessary authorisations/consents.

5. Data security (Art. 8 FADP; DPO Art. 1–3, SR 235.11)

  1. Appropriate technical/organisational measures: encryption in transit and at rest, access control, segregation, backup, integrity, availability, pseudonymisation where possible.
  2. Logging and processing regulations where required (DPO Art. 4–5).

6. Sub-processors (Art. 9 para. 3 FADP)

  1. List of sub-processors (hosting, OCR, AI services) in Appendix A; obligation to impose equivalent contractual commitments.
  2. General authorisation with advance notice and right of objection of the Fiduciary in the event of changes.
  3. AI tools. Arvut uses AI-supported tools (currently Anthropic PBC — «Claude Code») as sub-processors for the accounting production. The option to use the data for training/improving the models is deactivated and, where technically available, zero data retention (Zero Data Retention) is activated, without human review of the inputs beyond the residual abuse monitoring; merely deactivating training is not sufficient (a DPA with the provider + the transfer safeguard, clause 7, apply cumulatively). The AI output is always subject to human review (responsibility remains with Arvut). Additional AI providers are permitted only under the same conditions. Data of mandates subject to professional secrecy (Art. 321 SCC, e.g. audit) is transmitted to the AI tools only in anonymised form.

7. Cross-border transfers (Art. 16–17 FADP)

  1. By default, payroll data/sensitive personal data is processed in Switzerland or in countries with an adequate level of protection (list of the Federal Council, DPO Annex 1).
  2. For countries without adequacy: standard contractual clauses recognised by the FDPIC + transfer impact assessment + additional measures (encryption with keys held in Switzerland).

8. Data security breaches (Art. 24 FADP)

  1. Arvut informs the Fiduciary without undue delay of any breach, with the information needed to enable the Fiduciary to fulfil its obligations towards the FDPIC/the data subjects.

9. Assistance to the controller

  1. Assistance with the rights of the data subjects (access Art. 25, rectification, erasure, data portability Art. 28).
  2. Assistance with the data protection impact assessment (Art. 22) and the consultation of the FDPIC where required.

10. Return / deletion

  1. Upon termination, Arvut, at the Fiduciary's choice, returns the data (cf. Art. 8 SoW) or deletes it and certifies the deletion, subject to statutory retention obligations (e.g. Art. 958f CO, 10 years) — stating who keeps the archive.

11. Audit and demonstration of compliance

  1. Arvut makes available the information necessary to demonstrate compliance and allows reasonable audits.

12. Record of processing activities (Art. 12 FADP)

  1. Each party keeps its own record; the SME exemption may not apply in the case of large-scale processing of sensitive personal data.
