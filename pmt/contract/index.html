1) Terms and definitions

Mandate: an end-client of P&M whose accounting production is handled by Arvut under this Agreement (Annex 1). End-client: a natural or legal person with whom P&M has a mandate relationship for accounting services. Document / Voucher: a single document treated as a processing unit. Booking (Buchung): a single entry in the journal.

Accounting Revenue of a mandate: the portion of the annual fee invoiced by P&M to the end-client that is attributable solely to accounting production (bookkeeping) under this Agreement, excluding payroll/salaries, advisory and every other service rendered by P&M. It is the basis of the share under art. 7. VAT excluded. (Example: total client fee ~CHF 18'000/year, of which bookkeeping CHF 6'000, payroll ~CHF 5'000–8'000, advisory/other the remainder → the 50/50 share applies only to the CHF 6'000.)

Excluded services: payroll/salaries, advisory and every other service that is not accounting production remain within the competence — and revenue — of P&M and do not enter the calculation basis. Payroll may be included as a separate line, with its own basis and share, only if and when Arvut provides its production (addendum).

Arvut share = full compensation. 50% of the Accounting Revenue is the sole compensation of Arvut for the mandate and replaces every separate tariff for production on the ABM platform (in particular the CHF 500/year + CHF 1.50/booking tariff of the standard model, which does not apply to this Agreement). No cost is deducted before the split.

ABM Platform: the software platform owned exclusively by Arvut (art. 8). White-label: Arvut's services are rendered under the branding of P&M. Framework agreement: this Agreement governs uniformly all mandates, current and future, listed in Annex 1; mandates are added or removed by an update of Annex 1 countersigned by the Parties, without renegotiating or amending this Agreement. The terms agreed here are the standard model of the Arvut ↔ P&M collaboration and apply automatically to every new mandate entered in Annex 1.

Value-added services (real-time): the optional modules listed in Annex 3 (automatic document capture — portal, Telegram, tills/POS, bank via EBICS; real-time accounting; daily reporting per point of sale / cost and profit centres; automatic VAT eCH-0217; own white-label domain), activatable per mandate. They do not enter the Accounting Revenue or the 50/50 share; their remuneration is governed by Annex 3 (art. 7.6).

2) Collaboration model and nature of the agreement

The Parties collaborate in the management of the mandates: P&M is the front-end (client relationship, fiduciary licence, communications with authorities, VAT filing, annual closing and taxation, invoicing of the end-client); Arvut is the back-end of accounting production on the ABM platform.

Nature of the agreement. The Parties qualify this Agreement as a bilateral exchange-of-performance contract of continuing nature (Dauerschuldverhältnis) of mixed type (contract for services + grant of access to the platform). The Parties expressly exclude the formation of a simple partnership within the meaning of art. 530 et seq. of the Swiss Code of Obligations («CO»): (a) they pursue no common purpose but their own, opposing interests typical of an exchange relationship; (b) they contribute to and constitute no common assets (Gesamthandsvermögen); (c) each Party acts in its own name and for its own account, bears its own costs and risks exclusively and is not liable for the obligations of the other; (d) there is no animus societatis, no reciprocal power of representation and no joint management; (e) the determination of the remuneration under art. 7 is a contractual method of price calculation (variable consideration of the exchange), not a participation in the profits of a common enterprise. No provision grants a Party control, voting or veto rights over the business decisions of the other. P&M commissions the mandates and Arvut accepts them; there are no joint management decisions.

Termination. The Parties understand this relationship as a Dauerschuldverhältnis of predominantly exchange/contract-for-services character, to which art. 404 CO does not apply. In the alternative, should an authority hold art. 404 CO applicable: (i) the Parties acknowledge the mandatory nature of the right to terminate at any time; (ii) the Party terminating before the end of the current contractual period pays the other, as agreed liquidation of the damage (art. 404 para. 2 and art. 160 CO), an amount equal to 100% of the residual share until the end of the current contractual period, except in the case of termination for good cause attributable to the other Party.

Communication (default): clarifications Arvut → P&M → end-client. Direct contact Arvut ↔ end-client only with the prior written authorisation of P&M.

3) Scope of services (deliverables) — per mandate

Arvut provides for each mandate: postings at line/item level; open-items schedule and payment reconciliation (AR/AP); VAT calculation basis and reports ready for filing by P&M; preparation of the annual closing (preparation, not legal finalisation); integrated document ↔ posting archive (audit trail). Legal/tax finalisation and signature remain with P&M.

4) Input and process

P&M makes available supporting documents, bank statements/transaction data, proofs of payment; where necessary cash/expense records. The SLAs run from receipt of the complete set; in the case of missing data Arvut prepares an exception list. No cost is deducted from the Accounting Revenue before the split under art. 7 (the 50/50 share is gross and includes, for Arvut, every production fee — cf. art. 1, «Arvut share = full compensation»).

5) SLA and corrections

Standard processing: up to 2 weeks from receipt of the complete set. Corrections: within 2 business days from notification and confirmation by P&M.

6) Bookkeeping rules (P&M standard)

Arvut applies: (a) no rounding of posting amounts (the document amount is carried to the cent: 99.99 remains 99.99); (b) periods already filed/closed are not modified: documents received after the closing are posted in the first open period thereafter; for certain mandates the closing is monthly.

7) Determination of the remuneration (variable 50/50 share)

  1. The share of each Party is 50% of the Accounting Revenue of the mandate (art. 1), i.e. of the bookkeeping component of the client fee only — not of the total client fee, not of payroll, not of advisory. The share is gross: no cost is deducted and, for Arvut, it replaces every separate production tariff (no CHF 500 + 1.50/booking). For each mandate the base Accounting Revenue is stated in Annex 1.
  2. Periodicity: calculation and settlement quarterly, with a transparent statement (revenue, direct costs per Party, share of each).
  3. Invoicing of the end-client: P&M invoices the end-client the entire fee of the mandate (front-end / owner of the client relationship); Arvut invoices P&M its share (50% of the Accounting Revenue) at the periodicity of art. 7.2. Payment of the shares therefore occurs by invoicing of Arvut's share to P&M (VAT excluded, where due).
  4. Related-party transparency: where a direct cost is rendered by a related party of one of the Parties, the relevant tariff is disclosed transparently in the statement.
  5. In case of late payment between the Parties: default interest of 5% per annum (art. 104 CO) from the due date plus reminder costs. Amounts VAT excluded.
  6. Value-added services (Annex 3) — separate remuneration. Value-added services are remunerated separately under Annex 3 and do not enter the 50/50 basis. The price applied to the end-client for such services is determined freely and exclusively by P&M (front-end / owner of the client relationship); the remuneration due to Arvut is a wholesale tariff per module and per mandate, agreed in writing by the Parties on activation (annotation in Annex 1 or countersigned addendum), independent of the price charged to the client. The spread between the client price and the Arvut tariff is P&M's margin. Arvut does not intervene in, and has no claim on, the end-client price.

8) Ownership of the ABM platform (red line)

The ABM platform and every related intellectual-property right remain the exclusive, 100% property of Arvut. This Agreement concerns exclusively the operational collaboration and the variable remuneration, and grants P&M no right, share, licence of use (beyond the management access under art. 9), purchase option or title in or to the platform, the code, the aggregated data or the know-how of Arvut. The determination of the remuneration (art. 7) compensates exclusively the respective operational services and in no case constitutes consideration for a participation, licence or right of P&M in the platform. Termination of this Agreement gives rise to no residual, liquidation or goodwill claim of P&M on the platform or its value. Nothing in this Agreement constitutes a company, a joint venture or common assets.

9) Portal and white-label access

P&M has management access per mandate. End-clients may receive read-only access under the branding of P&M, if provided for in P&M's agreement with the client.

10) Remuneration review (review clause)

The economic terms of art. 7 are renegotiable at the request of either Party upon the following triggers: (a) after the first 5 active mandates, or (b) after 12 months from commencement. The renegotiation takes account of the actual evolution of roles and costs. Absent agreement on new terms, the current terms remain in force until the end of the contractual period. This clause is a price-adjustment mechanism of the exchange and implies no joint management or decision-making.

11) Handover / export

On request, Arvut provides structured exports (CSV/XLS or agreed formats). Upon termination: export of movements/data, PDF archive of documents and principal reports, support for the transfer into P&M's system (reasonable effort).

12) Liability (cap)

Arvut's liability per mandate is limited to the amount of the net shares received by Arvut for that mandate in the 12 months preceding the damaging event (rolling 12 months). This limitation does not apply in case of unlawful intent or gross negligence of Arvut (art. 100 para. 1 CO). For the conduct of its auxiliaries, Arvut is liable within the limits of art. 101 CO; this limitation does not cover unlawful intent or gross negligence of the auxiliaries. Liability for indirect damage, lost profit and consequential damage is excluded, save for unlawful intent or gross negligence.

13) Early termination and setup indemnity

In case of early termination by P&M before the end of the period, the accrued shares remain due together with a lump-sum indemnity covering the implementation/setup costs of CHF 1'000 per active mandate (flat-rate cost recovery, not a penalty). Extraordinary termination for good cause attributable to Arvut remains reserved, in which case no indemnity is due.

14) No poaching of clients (non-circumvention / non-solicitation) and penalty

Arvut acknowledges that all end-clients handled under this Agreement are clients of P&M.

  • 14.1 Non-circumvention. Arvut undertakes, for the entire term and for 24 months after termination, and limited to the end-clients actually introduced or handled under this Agreement, for accounting services of the same type as those covered by this Agreement, not to conclude or perform, directly or through third parties/affiliated companies, contracts with such clients. Arvut remains free to offer its services to any other person, fiduciary or client not handled under this Agreement. The undertaking is construed in accordance with art. 27 para. 2 of the Swiss Civil Code; where held excessive, it remains valid to the maximum extent permitted by law (art. 20 para. 2 CO).
  • 14.2 Non-solicitation. Arvut carries out no direct marketing, offers or solicitations aimed at moving P&M's clients to a direct contract with Arvut.
  • 14.3 Exception (spontaneous contact). If a client of P&M contacts Arvut spontaneously, Arvut: (a) informs P&M within a reasonable time; (b) proceeds exclusively through P&M, or concludes a direct contract only with the prior written authorisation of P&M.
  • 14.4 Penalty. For each breach of arts. 14.1–14.2, and for each mandate concerned, Arvut pays P&M a contractual penalty equal to 200% of the accounting fee of the mandate for the 12 months preceding the breach. As the breach concerns an obligation to abstain, the penalty is due in addition to cessation of the prohibited conduct (art. 160 para. 2 CO). P&M may claim damages exceeding the penalty (art. 161 para. 2 CO). The court's power to reduce an excessive penalty (art. 163 para. 3 CO), which the Parties acknowledge as mandatory, remains reserved.
  • 14.5 Reciprocity. The non-circumvention and non-solicitation obligations of arts. 14.1 and 14.2 are reciprocal: for the entire term of the Agreement and for 24 months from termination, P&M likewise refrains from soliciting or hiring Arvut's personnel and from circumventing Arvut with other suppliers in order to replicate, for the mandates covered by this Agreement, the accounting-production services and the modules of Annex 3, without prejudice to each Party's right to deal freely with third parties not handled under this Agreement. The contractual penalty of art. 14.4 applies mirror-wise against the defaulting Party. The reciprocal and balanced undertaking is construed in accordance with art. 27 para. 2 of the Swiss Civil Code.

15) Confidentiality and secrecy

Each Party treats the information of the other Party and of the end-clients as a business secret (art. 162 of the Swiss Criminal Code, «SCC»); the obligations last for 5 years from termination. Upon termination, subject to statutory retention duties, Arvut returns/destroys residual copies on written request. Arvut acts as an auxiliary person (Hilfsperson) of P&M; the professional secrecy of P&M remains unaffected (incl. art. 321 SCC where applicable); P&M warrants the necessary authorisations/consents.

15bis) Operational scope and secrecy

(a) Scope (AMLA line). Arvut performs accounting production exclusively. Arvut does not receive, hold or transfer client assets, holds no power of attorney over bank accounts and executes no payments: payments are only prepared (art. 7.6 / Annex 3, module E) and signed and executed by the client. Arvut is therefore not a financial intermediary within the meaning of the Anti-Money-Laundering Act (AMLA/GwG) and the activity is not subject to FINMA supervision.

(b) Secrecy. Arvut's personnel are bound by business secrecy (art. 162 SCC) and — where a mandate is subject to professional secrecy (art. 321 SCC, e.g. audit mandates) or bank secrecy (art. 47 of the Banking Act) — by the corresponding obligations, on a back-to-back basis without attenuation. Arvut assumes full responsibility for its auxiliary personnel (art. 101 CO).

(c) Carve-out. Should a mandate become an audit mandate (art. 321 SCC) or concern a supervised client (bank/FINMA-licensed institution — art. 47 Banking Act / FINMA Circ. 2018/3 Outsourcing), the corresponding additional regimes apply, to be agreed in writing.

15ter) Artificial-intelligence tools and sub-processors

  1. The principal (controller) authorises Arvut, as processor (art. 9 of the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection, «FADP»), to use artificial-intelligence-based tools (currently Anthropic PBC — «Claude Code») as a sub-processor for the performance of the accounting services. The current list of sub-processors is made available; the principal may object within 30 days to any new addition (art. 9 para. 3 FADP).
  2. Arvut warrants that the option to use the data for model training/improvement is disabled and, where technically available, Zero Data Retention is enabled, with no human review of the inputs beyond residual anti-abuse control. Disabling training alone is not sufficient: the DPA and the settings indicated here apply cumulatively.
  3. Arvut and the sub-processor are bound to secrecy (art. 162 SCC) and adopt technical/organisational measures (art. 8 FADP), including — where possible — minimisation/pseudonymisation of data requiring special protection (payslips) before transmission. The AI output is always subject to human review; responsibility remains with Arvut/P&M (the AI is a tool, not a responsible actor).
  4. Recourse to other AI providers is permitted only on the same conditions (DPA + entry/authorisation among the sub-processors + training disabled + minimal retention).
  5. Data of any audit mandates (art. 321 SCC) are not transmitted to the AI tools, save in anonymised form or with reinforced safeguards.

16) Final provisions

Written form for amendments; severability; no assignment without consent (save to group companies or in a reorganisation of Arvut); set-off only with undisputed or adjudicated claims; force majeure (SLAs suspended); notices in writing; electronic signature in counterparts. Arts. 8, 12, 13, 14, 15, 15bis, 15ter, 17 and Annex 2 survive termination.

17) Governing law and jurisdiction

This Agreement is governed by Swiss law, excluding its conflict-of-laws rules and the Vienna Convention (CISG). The exclusive place of jurisdiction for every dispute arising out of or in connection with this Agreement is Zug (seat of Arvut) (art. 17 of the Swiss Civil Procedure Code), subject to mandatory statutory fora.

This Agreement cancels and replaces every prior understanding relating to its subject matter, including earlier drafts. Amendments are valid only in writing.

Annex 4 — Client acquisition (referral programme «/best»)

Optional (opt-in) Annex to the Agreement. It applies exclusively if countersigned by both Parties at the foot of this Annex; non-signature in no way prejudices the Agreement. Integral part of the Agreement: in case of conflict between this Annex and the body of the Agreement, this Annex prevails for the matters governed here (art. 18 of the Agreement).

1) Subject, background and qualification

Arvut operates a demand channel through which Swiss businesses ask to be matched with a fiduciary of the Arvut network (currently via the page arvut.ch/best and the connected marketing channels; the channel may evolve without amending this Annex). This Annex governs the referral of prospective end-clients to P&M, the related acquisition fee and the relationship with art. 14 of the Agreement.

No volume commitment. Arvut guarantees no number, frequency or quality of Referrals; P&M is not obliged to accept any Referral. The matching programme is not exclusive: Arvut remains free to work with other fiduciaries of the network.

Qualification and transparency (art. 412 et seq. CO). The Parties qualify each Referral as brokerage within the meaning of art. 412 et seq. CO, in the framework relationship constituted by the Agreement. Arvut declares that it receives no brokerage fee from the prospect and that it operates for the entire network, including its own fiduciary entity as residual recipient (backstop) of unplaced Referrals; P&M takes note and consents (art. 415 CO). This Annex does not alter the nature of the Agreement (art. 2) and constitutes no simple partnership, joint venture or joint management.

Lawfulness of collection. Arvut warrants that leads are collected lawfully (in particular in compliance with the FADP and art. 3 of the Unfair Competition Act); P&M is not responsible for collection practices prior to delivery of the Referral.

2) Definitions

Referral (Lead): a documented communication by which Arvut presents to P&M a prospective end-client interested in fiduciary/accounting services, with the data collected (in particular: sector, size, canton, language, stated needs).

Introduced Client: a prospective end-client that is the subject of an accepted Referral and subsequently signs a mandate with P&M.

Referral Register: a register kept by Arvut (source of the lead, delivery date, assigned fiduciary, outcome). The Register is the basis of counting and invoicing of the fee under art. 4; P&M may at any time request an extract for the part concerning it. The Register is retained for 10 years for invoiced Referrals (art. 958f CO); for Referrals that do not result in a mandate, the personal data of contact persons are deleted or anonymised after 24 months.

3) Referral process and acceptance

3.1 Matching. Arvut matches prospects to the fiduciaries of the network by the criteria: canton → language → sector/size/capacity. On equal criteria, assignment rotates. Assignment to Arvut's own fiduciary entity occurs only residually (backstop), where no fiduciary of the network matches the criteria or accepts the Referral.

3.2 Exclusivity of the Referral. Each Referral is assigned to one fiduciary only; Arvut does not offer the same prospect in parallel to other fiduciaries until the Referral is refused or lapses under art. 3.3.

3.3 Acceptance (SLA). The Referral is deemed received upon delivery to the e-mail address indicated by P&M (or via the ABM platform). P&M communicates acceptance or refusal within 2 business days of receipt; Arvut sends a reminder at least one business day before expiry of the term. Absent a response within the term, the Referral is deemed refused and Arvut is free to reassign it in the order of art. 3.1.

3.4 Pre-matching phase. Contacts between Arvut and the prospect prior to delivery of the Referral (needs gathering, qualification, demo) do not breach arts. 2 and 14 of the Agreement, the prospect not yet being a client of P&M. From delivery of the accepted Referral, communication with the prospect follows the model of art. 2 of the Agreement.

4) Client Acquisition Fee

4.1 For each Introduced Client, P&M pays Arvut a one-time fee of CHF 500.-- (VAT excluded), due upon signature of the mandate between P&M and the Introduced Client. No fee is due for Referrals that do not result in a mandate.

4.2 The fee under art. 4.1 covers acquisition costs and is independent of, and cumulative with, the variable share under art. 7 of the Agreement (50/50 on the Accounting Revenue), which applies to the Introduced Client's mandate once entered in Annex 1 of the Agreement. The acquisition fee enters neither the Accounting Revenue basis nor the 50/50 split.

4.3 The prices charged by P&M to the Introduced Client are determined freely by P&M; the base Accounting Revenue of the new mandate is stated in Annex 1 (art. 7.1 of the Agreement).

4.4 Pre-existing relationships. No fee is due if P&M demonstrates, within the term of art. 3.3, a documented pre-existing business relationship or contact with the prospect; in that case the prospect is not an Introduced Client and the Referral is without effect.

5) Registration of the mandate and network standards

5.1 The Introduced Client's mandate is entered in Annex 1 of the Agreement (countersigned update, art. 1 «Framework agreement») and handled on the ABM platform under the Agreement. Handling outside the platform requires the Parties' prior written agreement.

5.2 Towards Introduced Clients P&M maintains the network standards communicated to the public (real-time up-to-date accounting, full document–posting reconciliation, client portal under P&M's branding, data hosting in Switzerland compliant with the FADP, ten-year retention — art. 958f CO), to the extent such standards are actually delivered by Arvut in accordance with the Agreement. P&M's obligation under this art. 5.2 is limited to keeping the mandate on the platform and not obstructing delivery; P&M remains front-end and solely responsible towards the end-client under art. 2 of the Agreement.

6) Relationship with art. 14 of the Agreement (non-circumvention)

6.1 From signature of the mandate, the Introduced Client is for all purposes a client of P&M; the protections of art. 14 of the Agreement (incl. the penalty of art. 14.4) apply in full for the entire duration of the mandate. For Introduced Clients, the 24-month period of art. 14.1 of the Agreement does not apply after termination of the relevant mandate; for end-clients not referred by Arvut, art. 6.3 remains unaffected.

6.2 Derogation (reassignment). In partial derogation from art. 14.1 of the Agreement, Arvut is free to reassign an Introduced Client — to another fiduciary of the network or, residually in the order of art. 3.1, to its own fiduciary entity — without incurring any penalty, exclusively in the following cases:

  • (a) the Referral was refused or lapsed (art. 3.3);
  • (b) the mandate between P&M and the Introduced Client has ended; if the ending is attributable to a serious breach by Arvut under the Agreement, reassignment to Arvut's own fiduciary entity is excluded for 12 months;
  • (c) the Introduced Client spontaneously asks Arvut for a new match, by a written, documented request of the client kept in the Register; in that case Arvut informs P&M without delay and P&M has 10 business days from the communication to remedy the client relationship before any reassignment.

6.3 For clarity, the full protection of art. 14 of the Agreement — including the 24-month post-termination period — remains unchanged for all end-clients not referred by Arvut (P&M's own clients, including mandates already entered in Annex 1).

7) Presentation of P&M and prospect data (FADP)

7.1 Consent to presentation. P&M authorises Arvut to present to prospects, in the course of matching, its name, seat/canton, working languages, size and areas of competence, including the contact persons designated by P&M; P&M warrants that it has informed them. No public list/directory of the network's fiduciaries is published.

7.2 Prospect data. For the personal data of prospects, Arvut and P&M act as separate controllers (art. 5 lit. j FADP), each for its own purposes; the processing on instruction under Annex 2 of the Agreement does not apply to this phase. Arvut warrants that the privacy notice published on arvut.ch names the disclosure of data to the network's fiduciaries as a category of recipients (art. 19 para. 2 FADP); P&M fulfils its own information duty (art. 19 para. 3 FADP) at the latest on first contact with the prospect. Marketing-attribution data (UTM and similar) remain internal to Arvut and are not transferred to P&M. P&M uses the data received exclusively to assess and, in case of acceptance, manage the relationship with the prospect; in case of refusal of the Referral it deletes them within 30 days, and may retain, for at most 12 months, only the minimum data (prospect name, date, outcome) as proof of the refusal.

7.3 Security and data requiring special protection. Referrals do not as a rule contain personal data requiring special protection (art. 5 lit. c FADP); Arvut removes or masks such data if present in free-text fields before delivery. Delivery of Referrals occurs via the ABM platform or an encrypted channel (art. 8 FADP). The Parties inform each other without delay of any security breach concerning prospect data (art. 24 FADP).

8) Term and termination

8.1 This Annex follows the term of the Agreement. Either Party may additionally terminate this Annex alone, in writing, with 30 days' notice to the end of a month, without effect on the Agreement.

8.2 Upon termination of this Annex the accrued fees remain due (art. 4). Arts. 4 (accrued fees), 6 and 7.2–7.3 survive termination of this Annex and of the Agreement, for the survival period of art. 14 of the Agreement (art. 16 of the Agreement).

8.3 Arvut may amend the economic terms (art. 4) for Referrals delivered after the amendment takes effect, with 3 months' prior written notice; amendments have no retroactive effect. In case of amendment, P&M may terminate this Annex under art. 8.1 before it takes effect. This art. 8.3 is an agreed, limited derogation from the written-form clause of art. 16 of the Agreement; the amendment is communicated in writing.
