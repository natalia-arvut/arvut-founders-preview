Statement of Work

White-label accounting production — Arvut ↔ P&M CFO Treuhand · operational annex to the Collaboration and Revenue-Share Agreement

This Statement of Work («SoW») operationalises the Collaboration and Revenue-Share Agreement between the Parties (the «Framework Agreement»). In case of conflict, the Framework Agreement prevails (§16). Fields marked […] are to be completed on signature. Comments and mark-ups are welcome — this draft is prepared for discussion with P&M CFO Treuhand AG.

1) Terms and definitions

Mandate: an end-client of the Fiduciary served by Arvut under this SoW. End-client: a natural or legal person with whom the Fiduciary has a mandate/contract relationship for accounting services.

Document / Voucher / Source document: a single document or file treated as a processing unit (e.g. invoice, receipt/voucher, proof of payment, bank statement/report, contract/document where required, etc.). Booking (Buchung): a single entry in the journal. Bookings are the unit of production measurement; the remuneration itself is not charged per booking but follows the 50/50 revenue share of the Framework Agreement (§7).

White-label: Arvut's services are rendered under the branding of the Fiduciary, unless otherwise agreed in writing. Read-only access: access for consultation only (no editing or entry rights). Fiduciary's system (target system): the accounting system used by the Fiduciary for administration/finalisation and closing.

2) Collaboration model (white-label, front-end / back-end)

Arvut is a managed back-end accounting-production service in white-label mode.

The Fiduciary (front-end) is responsible for:

  • the relationship with the end-clients and the contracts;
  • communication with authorities/bodies, including filings;
  • VAT filing on the basis of the material prepared by Arvut;
  • annual closing, accrual/closing entries, statutory accounts, taxes.

Arvut (back-end) is responsible for:

  • processing of documents and data, posting/bookings, reconciliations;
  • preparation of the VAT calculation basis and VAT reports;
  • preparation of the reporting packages for the Fiduciary under this SoW.

Nature of the contract. The qualification of the relationship — a bilateral exchange-of-performance contract of continuing nature (Dauerschuldverhältnis) of mixed type, with express exclusion of a simple partnership (art. 530 et seq. CO) and the agreed treatment of art. 404 CO — is governed by art. 2 of the Framework Agreement, which applies to this SoW.

Communication (default): queries via Arvut → Fiduciary → end-client. Direct contact Arvut ↔ end-client only with the prior written authorisation of the Fiduciary.

3) Scope of services (deliverables) — per mandate

Arvut provides for each mandate:

  • Line-item posting: processing of the documents; detailed bookings per line/item, not merely by totals.
  • Open items and payment reconciliation: due-date schedule / open items for customers and suppliers (AR/AP); allocation and reconciliation of payments on the basis of bank statements / transaction data / payment confirmations.
  • VAT: calculation basis and report, ready for filing by the Fiduciary.
  • Preparation of the annual closing (preparation, not legal finalisation): balance sheet / income statement, open items, VAT report in PDF; package ready for review and finalisation by the Fiduciary.
  • Integrated archive (document ↔ posting): traceable document–booking linkage for fast checks and audit trail.

4) Input and process

The Fiduciary makes available for each mandate: vouchers, bank statements/transaction data, proofs of payment; where required cash/expense records.

The SLA periods run from receipt of the complete set for the reporting period. In the case of missing data, Arvut prepares a query/exception list for the Fiduciary.

5) SLA and corrections

  • Standard processing: up to 2 weeks from receipt of the complete set;
  • Corrections: within 2 business days from notification and confirmation by the Fiduciary.

6) Portal and white-label access

  • the Fiduciary has management access per mandate;
  • end-clients may receive read-only access under the branding of the Fiduciary (white-label), where provided for in the Fiduciary's agreement with the client.

7) Remuneration (50/50 revenue share — no per-booking tariff)

The remuneration for the services under this SoW is governed exclusively by art. 7 of the Framework Agreement: each Party's share is 50% of the Accounting Revenue of the mandate (bookkeeping component of the client fee only; payroll, advisory and other services of the Fiduciary excluded), settled quarterly with a transparent statement; Arvut invoices its share to the Fiduciary (VAT excluded, where due).

For clarity: no base subscription, no per-booking tariff and no volume discounts apply under this SoW — the standard Arvut model (CHF 500/year per mandate + CHF 1.50/booking) is replaced by the 50/50 share (art. 1 and art. 7.1 of the Framework Agreement, «Arvut share = full compensation»). No minimum package and no implementation fee apply. In case of late payment: default interest of 5% per annum (art. 104 CO) from the due date plus reasonable reminder costs.

Value-added services (real-time) are optional modules per Annex 3 of the Framework Agreement, remunerated separately at the wholesale tariff agreed in writing on activation (art. 7.6 of the Framework Agreement); they never enter the 50/50 basis.

8) Handover / export into the Fiduciary's system

On request, Arvut supplies structured exports (CSV/XLS and/or agreed formats) for handover and reconciliation in the system used by the Fiduciary (target system).

Upon termination of the collaboration, Arvut supplies:

  • export of the movements/data;
  • PDF archive of the documents and of the principal reports;
  • support for the transfer/import into the target system (reasonable effort).

9) Liability (cap)

Arvut's liability is governed by art. 12 of the Framework Agreement: per mandate, it is limited to the net shares received by Arvut for that mandate in the 12 months preceding the damaging event (rolling 12 months); the limitation does not apply in case of unlawful intent or gross negligence of Arvut or its auxiliaries (art. 100 para. 1 and art. 101 CO), nor where the law prohibits a limitation. Liability for indirect damage, lost profit and consequential damage is excluded, save for unlawful intent or gross negligence.

10) Early termination and setup indemnity

Early termination and the lump-sum setup indemnity of CHF 1'000 per active mandate (flat-rate cost recovery, not a penalty) are governed by art. 13 of the Framework Agreement. Extraordinary termination for good cause attributable to Arvut remains reserved, in which case no indemnity is due.

11) Mandate annexes and admission of mandates

The list of mandates is maintained as Annex 1 of the Framework Agreement (single mandate register — no separate register is kept under this SoW). Mandates are added or removed by a countersigned update of that Annex; the admission of mandates does not alter the other terms of this SoW (SLA, remuneration model, operating model), unless expressly stated in the relevant update.

12) No poaching of clients (non-circumvention / non-solicitation)

The non-circumvention and non-solicitation undertakings, the exception for spontaneous client contact, the contractual penalty and the reciprocity of those obligations are governed by art. 14 of the Framework Agreement, which applies in full to all end-clients served under this SoW.

13) Confidentiality and secrecy

Confidentiality, business secrecy (art. 162 SCC), the position of Arvut as auxiliary person of the Fiduciary, professional secrecy (art. 321 SCC where applicable), the operational scope (AMLA line — no receipt, holding or transfer of client assets, no bank power of attorney, no payment execution) and the use of AI tools as sub-processors are governed by arts. 15, 15bis and 15ter of the Framework Agreement. Arvut may disclose to its auxiliaries/subcontractors only where these are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations, and remains responsible for them (art. 101 CO).

14) Final provisions

  • Written form: amendments and supplements are valid only if agreed in writing by both Parties; this written-form clause itself may be amended only in writing.
  • Severability: the invalidity or unenforceability of a clause does not affect the others; the Parties replace it by one with equivalent, legally permissible economic effect.
  • Assignment: neither Party may assign this SoW without the written consent of the other, save for assignment to group companies or a reorganisation of Arvut.
  • Set-off: the Fiduciary may set off only undisputed or adjudicated claims.
  • Force majeure: neither Party is liable for non-performance due to force majeure (including network outages and non-attributable external events); the SLAs are suspended for its duration.
  • Notices: in writing (registered mail or e-mail to the addresses indicated by the Parties).
  • Electronic signature: this SoW may be signed electronically and in counterparts, each of which is an original.
  • Survival: §§9 (liability), 10 (indemnity), 12 (non-circumvention), 13 (confidentiality), 15 (governing law and jurisdiction) and the Annex — Data Processing survive termination.

15) Governing law and jurisdiction

This SoW is governed by Swiss law. The exclusive place of jurisdiction is Zug, in line with art. 17 of the Framework Agreement, subject to mandatory statutory fora.

16) Precedence and replacement of prior agreements

This SoW cancels and fully replaces every prior agreement, understanding, correspondence, proposal and annex between Arvut and the Fiduciary concerning the subject of back-end accounting services in white-label mode, unless expressly stated otherwise in this SoW or its annexes. This SoW is an operational annex to the Framework Agreement: in case of conflict between this SoW and the Framework Agreement, the Framework Agreement prevails. Amendments and supplements are valid only if agreed in writing by both Parties.

Annex — Data Processing Agreement (art. 9 FADP / SR 235.1)

Data Processing Agreement (Auftragsbearbeitungsvertrag). Integral part of this SoW; incorporated by reference as Annex 2 of the Framework Agreement. No processing of real end-client data takes place before this Annex is signed.

1. Roles and subject matter

  1. The Fiduciary is the controller (Verantwortlicher, art. 5 lit. j FADP); Arvut is the processor (Auftragsbearbeiter, art. 5 lit. k and art. 9 FADP).
  2. Arvut processes the data exclusively for the performance of the services of this SoW and on the documented instruction of the Fiduciary.
  3. The Fiduciary warrants that it is the controller (or an authorised processor) and holds the end-clients' authorisation to engage Arvut as (sub-)processor (art. 9 para. 3 FADP).

2. Data categories and data subjects

  1. Data subjects: employees, suppliers, customers of the mandates.
  2. Categories: master data, accounting data, bank data, payroll data (incl. AHV/BVG/withholding tax) and any data requiring special protection concerning health (e.g. sickness/accident benefits under the Accident Insurance Act) — art. 5 lit. c FADP.

3. Instructions and limits

  1. Processing only on instruction; no use for Arvut's own purposes (art. 9 para. 1 FADP).
  2. Arvut does only what the Fiduciary is itself entitled to do.
  3. Arvut reports instructions it considers unlawful.

4. Confidentiality and secrecy

  1. Arvut and its personnel are bound to confidentiality, also after termination; Arvut acts as auxiliary person of the Fiduciary.
  2. The professional secrecy of the Fiduciary, if any, remains unaffected (incl. art. 321 SCC where applicable); the Fiduciary warrants the necessary authorisations/consents.

5. Data security (art. 8 FADP; DPO arts. 1–3, SR 235.11)

  1. Appropriate technical/organisational measures: encryption in transit and at rest, access control, separation, backup, integrity, availability, pseudonymisation where possible.
  2. Logging and a processing policy where required (DPO arts. 4–5).

6. Sub-processors (art. 9 para. 3 FADP)

  1. List of sub-processors (hosting, OCR, AI services) in Appendix A; obligation to impose equivalent contractual commitments.
  2. General authorisation with advance notice and the Fiduciary's right to object to changes.
  3. AI tools. Arvut uses AI-based tools (currently Anthropic PBC — «Claude Code») as sub-processors for accounting production. The option to use the data for model training/improvement is disabled and, where technically available, Zero Data Retention is enabled, with no human review of the inputs beyond residual anti-abuse control; disabling training alone is not sufficient (a DPA with the provider applies in every case). The AI output is always subject to human review (responsibility remains with Arvut). Further AI providers are permitted only on the same conditions. Data of mandates subject to professional secrecy (art. 321 SCC, e.g. audit) are transmitted to the AI tools only in anonymised form.

7. Cross-border transfers (arts. 16–17 FADP)

  1. By default, payroll data / data requiring special protection are processed in Switzerland or in countries with adequate protection (Federal Council list, DPO Annex 1).
  2. For countries without adequacy: standard contractual clauses recognised by the FDPIC + transfer impact assessment + additional measures (encryption with keys in Switzerland).

8. Data-security breaches (art. 24 FADP)

  1. Arvut informs the Fiduciary without delay of any breach, with the information needed for the Fiduciary to fulfil its duties towards the FDPIC / the data subjects.

9. Assistance to the controller

  1. Assistance with data-subject rights (access art. 25, rectification, deletion, data portability art. 28).
  2. Assistance with the data-protection impact assessment (art. 22) and consultation of the FDPIC where required.

10. Return / deletion

  1. Upon termination, at the Fiduciary's choice, Arvut returns the data (cf. §8 SoW) or deletes them and certifies the deletion, subject to statutory retention duties (e.g. art. 958f CO, 10 years) — stating who keeps the archive.

11. Verification and evidence

  1. Arvut makes available the information necessary to demonstrate compliance and permits reasonable verifications.

12. Record of processing activities (art. 12 FADP)

  1. Each Party keeps its own record; the SME exemption may not apply where data requiring special protection are processed on a large scale.

Appendix A — List of sub-processors (name, service, seat/country, transfer safeguards). Includes in particular: hosting (CH); AI tools — Anthropic PBC, training disabled, Zero Data Retention where available.
