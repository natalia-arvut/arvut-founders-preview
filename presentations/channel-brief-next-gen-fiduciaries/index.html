Next-Generation Fiduciaries

Channel brief — founding new Treuhand firms on Arvut

For: Board discussion (Heinz, Federico, Alex)  ·  From: Arvut Strategy  ·  Date: 11 July 2026

Status: Proposal for decision — no external action taken or planned until the board approves.

1The thesis

Alongside (a) modernizing existing fiduciaries (Figemax → OBT → TBO pipeline) and (b) deep partnerships (Diacron), the P&M case opens a third GTM channel: find qualified, ambitious people and help them found new-generation Treuhand firms — born on Arvut infrastructure.

Why it's attractive: a new fiduciary has zero legacy (no old software, no ingrained processes) → 100% wallet share from day one. P&M proved the entry barrier is gone: profile, hub, white-label landing, market study, contracts and NDA — in one working day, before the company was even registered. And demographics work for us: a wave of retiring owners of classic Treuhand offices means mandates coming free that someone must pick up.

2Who we target (the pool)

Treuhand is a regulated profession — the «young and ambitious founder» here is not a startup-hub generalist but someone who holds the qualification yet lacks capital, clients, and infrastructure:

  1. Fresh qualification holders — Fachausweis Treuhänder / dipl. Treuhandexperte graduates (annual exam cohorts; reachable via associations and schools).
  2. Managers aged ~30–40 in Big-4 / mid-tier firms (BDO, Forvis Mazars, OBT-class) who have hit the partner-track ceiling. Their biggest fear about leaving — «who runs my back office?» — is exactly what we remove.
  3. Employees of retiring-owner firms with no successor — the hottest segment: an MBI scenario where the person and the freed-up mandates come as a package. They know the clients personally; they lack only the structure.

Startup incubators are explicitly out of scope: no licences there.

3How we reach them (the entry)

There is no existing «founders in fiduciary» scene — no events, no program. That gap is the opportunity: whoever names it, owns it.

  • Program, not conference: «Next-Generation Treuhand» — a cohort track: day-one infrastructure + founding support + first mandates. We define it; nobody else has.
  • Associations as a room, not a mailbox: TREUHAND|SUISSE sections and ExpertSuisse young-member circles — entered through people (Heinz's M&A network, Oliver's and Alberto's professional circles), not via info@ cold mail. First posture: a talk («how to open a Treuhand office without a back office — a one-day case»), not a pitch.
  • Schools: HWZ, ZHAW, FFHS Treuhand-Lehrgänge — a guest lecture with the same case is a legitimate, cheap entry to exactly segment 1.

5Economics — and the honest caveat

Pricing frame = open board decision. 50/50 gross feels natural for this channel (deep alignment, zero legacy) — but making 50/50 the default for a whole channel is a policy call, not a per-deal call. Alternative: standard 500 + 1.50 with an upgrade path to 50/50 once volume proves out. Decide once, explicitly. (Reminder of our own principle: we split revenue, never profit.)

The caveat: a new fiduciary has zero mandates → 50% of zero is zero. The channel only earns when paired with a mandate source: /best flow (not yet live) or succession purchases. So this channel is not a third pillar standing alone — it is the bridge between the retiring-owner wave and our infrastructure: new fiduciaries are the hands that pick up dying offices' mandates on ABM.

6Proof gate — when the channel counts as real

P&M is one swallow, and not yet registered. Before building the program publicly:

  1. P&M registered (real UID) and contract signed;
  2. P&M serving its first real paying client on ABM;
  3. /best delivering its first real leads (so «first mandates» in the offer is a fact, not a promise).

Until all three: internal preparation only (people mapping, talk outline, program naming). No association outreach, no public program announcement. Our own canon applies: sell what is PROVEN/LIVE.

7What we ask the board to decide

  1. Green-light the channel direction (yes / no / park).
  2. Pricing frame for channel partners: 50/50 gross default · standard 500+1.50 with upgrade path · case-by-case.
  3. Heinz & Federico's role in the founding-support module (§4) — are they in, and on what terms?
  4. Proof gate confirmation (§6) — agree that external moves wait for the three conditions.

One-line summary: opening new fiduciaries is easier than modernizing old ones — and P&M shows we can do it in a day. But the channel goes public only after its first case actually exists.
